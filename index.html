About Me

My name is Avery, and I'm currently studying Computer Science and Political Science at Carleton College.

I have noticed some trends affecting political discourse in America while helping advocate for politicians who I believe will help steer the country in a fair and just direction. There is an extremely important overlap between computer science and politics. The mediums through which the public recieves information about policy and participates in political discourse have shifted to an online format. This online format has led to the rise of rapid misinformation and polarization. Contrary to those popular computer stickers, this machine no longer kills fascists. This online format leads to separate media worlds in which people spend their digital time. As an aspiring computer programmer, I hope to begin bridging the ideological schism which divides our country.

In my free time, I enjoy learning how to code in new ways, running, and playing bass guitar. Currently, I run cross country and track at Carleton College. I ran my first marathon solo this Spring during the COVID-19 shutdown.
